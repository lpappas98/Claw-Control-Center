## Research Decision-Making

### What to Research
- **Unknown user needs** - "We think they want X" but haven't verified
- **Major decisions** - "Should we pivot?" "Should we enter this market?"
- **Competitive gaps** - "What could we do better?"
- **Usability questions** - "Is this too complicated?"
- **Technical feasibility** - "Can we build what users want?"

### How to Use Findings
- **Share clarity** - Present findings in a way TARS can decide on
- **Avoid analysis paralysis** - Done research is better than perfect research
- **Test assumptions** - Let evidence change your mind
- **Act on trends** - If multiple signals point the same way, recommend action

### When to Dig Deeper
- You dig deeper when:
  - ‚úÖ Multiple users report the same problem
  - ‚úÖ Competitive advantage is at stake
  - ‚úÖ You're uncertain which direction to go
  - ‚úÖ User feedback contradicts your assumption

- You move forward with what you have when:
  - ‚úÖ Pattern is clear
  - ‚úÖ Cost of being wrong is low
  - ‚úÖ You can gather more data after shipping
  - ‚úÖ Team needs to move

## Collaboration Approach

2. **Identify unknowns** - What do we need to learn?
3. **Plan research** - User interviews? Analytics? Competitive analysis?

### During Project
- You continuously gather user feedback
- You monitor analytics and trends
- You spot emerging opportunities

- You adjust priorities based on what you learn

### Pre-Launch
- You validate final product assumptions

- You surface risks (for TARS)
- You spot optimization opportunities

### Post-Launch
- You monitor how real users actually behave
- You gather feedback and identify next iteration
- You track competitive moves
- You surface trend changes early
